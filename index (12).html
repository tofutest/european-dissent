What Does It Mean to Be an Anti-Racist White Organizer in 2025?

Reflections from longtime members on accountability, community, and the ongoing work.

Being an anti-racist white organizer in 2025 means continually reckoning with the structures we were raised inside of ‚Äî and choosing, over and over, to work against them. It means showing up not as saviors, but as people willing to be accountable to communities we have historically harmed.

Longtime European Dissent members describe this as a practice of humility: learning to follow Black and brown leadership, to sit with discomfort rather than resolve it prematurely, and to hold space for mistakes without collapsing. The work is not glamorous. It is slow, relational, and deeply necessary.

What 2025 has clarified is that white anti-racist organizing must be embedded in real relationships ‚Äî not just ideology. Our chapter returns again and again to the question: who are we accountable to, and how do we show up when it costs us something?

Written by European Dissent Seattle members ¬∑ February 2025. Rooted in the PISAB (People's Institute for Survival and Beyond) Undoing Racism¬Æ framework.

History

The People's Institute and the Roots of European Dissent

How a national framework took local shape in the Pacific Northwest.

The People's Institute for Survival and Beyond developed the Undoing Racism¬Æ workshop framework in New Orleans in the 1980s, grounded in the idea that racism is not simply personal prejudice but a system maintained by institutions, history, and culture ‚Äî including white culture.

European Dissent grew out of that framework as a space for white people to do their own work. The Seattle chapter took shape in the early 2000s, as local organizers recognized that asking BIPOC communities to educate white people was itself a form of extractive labor. White people needed to organize each other.

Today, our chapter continues to be shaped by the PISAB (People's Institute for Survival and Beyond) principles: understanding the role of white institutions, developing principled leadership, and building accountable relationships across race.

Source: People's Institute for Survival and Beyond ¬∑ European Dissent Seattle chapter history.

Following, Not Leading: White Solidarity in Practice

How our chapter thinks about accountable support for BIPOC-led organizing.

One of the most important ‚Äî and most misunderstood ‚Äî principles in white anti-racist work is following rather than leading. This does not mean passivity. It means recognizing whose vision should be centered, whose analysis should guide strategy, and whose community is most directly impacted by any given struggle.

In practice, this looks like European Dissent showing up to support campaigns led by Got Green, OneAmerica, or the South Seattle Emerald ‚Äî bringing our networks, our time, and our resources without trying to shape the agenda. It means asking "what do you need from us?" rather than "here is what we can offer."

It also means doing internal work so that we do not bring our unexamined privilege into spaces of solidarity. The goal is not allyship as identity, but accountability as practice ‚Äî ongoing, imperfect, and essential.

Written by European Dissent Seattle members. In solidarity with Got Green and OneAmerica.
